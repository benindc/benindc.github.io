Biography

Hi! My name is Ben. I'm from Herndon, VA and a recent Virginia Tech graduate with a B.S. in Water: Resources, Policy & Management. During my time at Virginia Tech I worked for Dr. Peter Vikesland in the Virginia Tech Center for Sustainable Nanotechnology. Here I was first exposed to lab work and independent research by studying sorbing interactions between antibiotic resistant plasmids and nanoparticles. After graduating with the highest honors from Virginia Tech in 2019, I moved to Charlottesville to begin my M.S. in Civil Engineering. At UVA I joined Dean Benson's research group where up until the pandemic I was researching landfill liners specialized for the removal of radionuclides. Since the pandemic, I have decided to take a sabbatical from my Masters degree by working at Curative Inc., a COVID testing company, as a Clinical Lab Assistant running patient qPCR samples. I plan on returning to UVA when COVID calms down a bit.

Undergraduate Nanoscience Research

From the summer of 2018 up until I went to UVA in the Fall of 2019 I was fortunate enough to partake in undergraduate research with Dr. Peter Vikesland and now my good friend and PhD. to be, Ethan Boeding. Here is a summary of our research conducted over a year.

Bhola Cyclone Encyclopedia Entry

My 2nd year at Virginia Tech I took the class "Water, Hazards and Development" by Luke Juran. As a replacement to the final paper, a peer and myself were offered the opportunity to write an encyclopedia entry for publication on the Bhola Cyclone.

Multisorbent Barrier Paper

Upon my arrival to the University of Virginia, I immediately joined forces with some ongoing work on a multisorbing barrier specialized for radioactive waste. My collauges and I collaborated internationally on this conference paper that was published in March 2020.

Hydrology Final

For my final project in graduate level hydrology, I was interested in modeling a potential sewage break in the Rivanna River. I used this opportunity to familiarize myself with the history and economics of sanitation while also learning the basics to WASP, a water quality simulation program. I found the program to be very limited in its interpretation of bacterial lifecycle and transport in streams, creating a shortage of data I was willing to work with.

Water Resources Policy and Economics Final

Following a semester of learning different economic and policy principles related to water resources, myself and peers investigated the west coast drought in a policy recommendation document.
